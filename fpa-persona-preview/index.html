What we build and how it works

Agents are the part of the system that does the work. Think of each one as a capable colleague for a slice of your job, briefed on how you work and pointed at your own files and tools. An agent is broad, not a single trick. A sales agent, for example, can draft proposals, prepare for meetings, write follow-ups, review the pipeline and research a prospect, all under one roof. We typically build two or three agents customised to your biggest time-sinks, and every system also comes with a set of standard agents included: a daily brief, a support agent that helps you run the system and keep it healthy, a knowledge keeper that files and organises what you feed it, and a meeting assistant that turns recorded calls into write-ups and actions. Your agents draw on your operating system as they work, so they answer with your context and priorities, not generic advice. That combination, your knowledge plus agents built for your jobs, is what makes the capability specific to your business rather than off-the-shelf. Each platform also has a library of ready-made agents you can download and adapt, and the programme shows you how to find, install and shape them, and how to update and fine-tune your own over time so they keep improving. A quick note on names, since the platforms differ: what we call an agent is a skill in Claude, a workspace agent or custom GPT in ChatGPT for Work, and an agent in Copilot Studio. Same idea, different label. If you later need more agents, or more complex bespoke ones, including agents that run multi-step workflows or interact with your customers, suppliers and partners, we can build those as an add-on, scoped and priced separately.

Dashboards are your at-a-glance view of what matters, but they do more than show numbers. Each one reads your latest information and adds a short plain-language summary of what is going on, plus two or three recommended actions, so it is closer to a quick read from a good analyst than a wall of charts. You get a main command centre that pulls the day together, plus role views for the things you track most, such as your pipeline, your projects, your team's activity or the health of the system itself. They work in all three platforms, with small differences in how they are presented and refreshed. The file system is the heart of your operating system: a tidy, organised set of folders holding your company and role knowledge, your templates, your daily briefs and task list, and the working files your agents read and write. It is structured around how you actually work, so the layout is yours, not a generic template. Your system can read, write and create real files in the formats you already use, including Word, Excel, PowerPoint, PDF, Markdown, Google Docs and many others. On Claude Cowork and ChatGPT for Work the file system lives directly on your own Mac or PC and syncs to the cloud storage you already use, such as OneDrive, Google Drive or iCloud Drive. That keeps it backed up and portable, and it is how a team shares a common knowledge base while each person keeps their own space. Microsoft Copilot 365 works differently but achieves the same thing, sitting more naturally within SharePoint and the Microsoft 365 files you already have. Who can view, edit or track changes to each folder follows the security and permissions of the system it is stored on, such as your SharePoint or Google Workspace setup, managed by your IT team. So company-wide information can be readable by everyone but editable only by approved people, team folders can be limited to the team, and personal folders to just you. Whichever platform you are on, the system can also reach into your wider files where you allow it, searching across OneDrive, Google Drive or SharePoint so your agents can work from documents you already keep there.

Connectors are what let your system reach the tools you already use, so it works with real information rather than only what you paste in. As part of the programme we help you connect the essentials, typically your email and calendar plus one other key system your team relies on that has a standard connector, such as your CRM. We do this with you and, on the team and company programmes, alongside your IT team. For security reasons we guide and support the connection but we don't make it for you: enabling access to your own systems is rightly IT's call. What a connector can actually do depends on both the system it connects to and the AI platform, and the three differ here. Reading your calendar or drafting an email is common, while sending on your behalf may be limited or switched off by default, which is usually what you want. We walk through exactly what is possible for your setup on the programme. More complex connections are possible too, using tools like APIs, MCP or Microsoft Power Automate, and we can build those as a separately scoped add-on.
