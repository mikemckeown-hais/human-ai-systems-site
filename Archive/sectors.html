Sectors

The sector context shapes everything.

The underlying challenge of AI adoption is often similar. But the governance environment, the pace of change, the workforce reality and the accountability structures are different in every sector — and that difference matters for how the work gets done.

Sector

Local Government

Democratic accountability, constrained budgets, and a workforce ranging from digitally confident to cautious make AI adoption in local government a genuinely different challenge. Governance isn't optional — it's fundamental to everything. Mike has worked at Cabinet Member level and led AI adoption directly inside a council.

Sector

Central Government & Public Services

NHS trusts, central government departments, police, housing associations, and universities share many of the governance characteristics of local government — public accountability, procurement constraints, union considerations — while operating at different scales and with different mandates. The AI opportunity across these organisations is significant and largely untapped.

Sector

Financial Services

Strong regulatory requirements mean AI has to be embedded carefully — into controlled workflows, with auditability, and with governance that satisfies FCA and model risk standards. The opportunity is significant. So is the cost of getting governance wrong after the fact.

Sector

Professional Services & Technology

Knowledge-intensive teams can move quickly with AI — but only when workflow design, accountability, client confidentiality and quality controls are thought through properly. The Verimatrix AI transformation programme is a direct reference point: an engineering team redesigned around AI, with governance and productivity gains that went to board level.

Sector

Small & Medium Organisations

Smaller organisations often have clearer AI opportunities and fewer layers of complexity — but also less internal capacity to pursue adoption properly. Engagements can be scoped to be right-sized: a focused diagnostic, a narrow pilot, or fractional senior leadership for a leadership team that doesn't yet have AI expertise in-house.

The common thread

What stays the same across every sector

The sector context changes the constraints. The underlying work — identifying genuine opportunities, testing them in real operations, and embedding what works — stays the same.

Start with real workflows

AI adoption that isn't grounded in how work actually happens produces pilots that work and programmes that don't. Every sector has a different workflow reality.

Governance is a design problem

Every sector has a different governance environment — FCA, democratic accountability, professional liability. Well-designed governance enables adoption in all of them.

Evidence before commitment

The Radar model exists because organisations make better decisions when they start with honest assessment. That applies whether you're a council, a bank, or a ten-person firm.
