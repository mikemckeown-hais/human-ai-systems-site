01
Discovery & Assessment

AI Adoption Radar

"Most AI discussions start with the technology. The right question is: where in your operations is AI actually going to create value — and where might it create risk you haven't accounted for?"

The Radar is a structured discovery and assessment process that maps how your organisation actually works — workflows, decision points, data flows, governance boundaries — and identifies where AI can create genuine value.

It surfaces the realistic opportunities, not the aspirational ones. It identifies governance requirements before you've committed to anything. And it gives you a clear, prioritised recommendation on where to start.

Most engagements begin here. The output becomes the foundation for everything that follows — whether that's a pilot programme or a conversation with your board.

What you get

  • Workflow opportunity map — where AI can create real value, ranked by impact and feasibility
  • Governance risk assessment — where oversight, accountability and compliance requirements apply
  • AI maturity assessment — an honest picture of where you are today
  • Prioritised pilot recommendation — a clear starting point with rationale
  • Executive briefing — board or leadership-ready summary of findings
Typical duration

2–4 weeks

Who it's for

Organisations exploring AI and wanting evidence before commitment

02
Structured Testing

Pilot Programmes

"Buying an AI tool is easy. Getting it to change how work actually happens is not."

A Pilot Programme takes a specific workflow — either identified in a Radar or already on your radar — and tests AI properly inside real operations. Not a proof of concept in isolation. A live test with real work, real people, and real accountability.

The pilot is designed with clear success measures from the start. It includes workflow redesign to accommodate AI, governance controls appropriate to the context, and structured learning throughout. At the end, you have evidence — not just anecdote.

Pilots can be focused on a single workflow or span a team or department. They are always designed with scaling in mind: the question isn't just "does this work?" but "could this work at scale?"

What you get

  • Pilot blueprint — scope, workflow integration plan, success measures, governance controls
  • Baseline and outcome measurement — documented before and after
  • Workflow redesign — how the work changes when AI is part of it
  • Governance framework — oversight, accountability, escalation paths
  • Scaling recommendation — a clear evidence-based case for what comes next
Typical duration

6–12 weeks

Who it's for

Organisations with a clear opportunity who want to test it properly before scaling

03
Operating Model Change

Scale & Operating Model

"A successful pilot is not the same as a transformed organisation."

When pilots work, the question becomes: how do we make this permanent? Scaling AI isn't about rolling out more tools. It's about redesigning how the organisation works — workflows, accountability structures, capability and governance — so that AI becomes a reliable part of operations, not a parallel experiment.

This is the hardest part of AI adoption. It requires genuine operating model change: leaders who understand how human-AI collaboration works, teams who are capable and confident, and governance that is rigorous without being obstructive.

Scale & Operating Model engagements are typically built on the foundation of a completed pilot, though they can also work from existing internal evidence. The output is a redesigned way of working that is sustainable without ongoing consultancy support.

What you get

  • AI operating model — target state for how AI sits within the organisation's workflows
  • Governance framework — human oversight, accountability, escalation, review cycles
  • Redesigned workflows — with AI roles clearly defined and human judgement preserved
  • Capability programme — what teams and leaders need to know and be able to do
  • Leadership toolkit — how to manage, measure and develop AI-augmented teams
Typical duration

3–6 months

Who it's for

Organisations ready to move beyond pilots and embed AI into how they permanently operate

04
Senior Advisory

Fractional AI Leadership

"Not every organisation needs a full-time Chief AI Officer. Most benefit from senior AI leadership — board credibility, governance discipline, someone who's done this before."

Fractional AI Leadership provides experienced senior oversight for your AI programme on a retained basis. This means working alongside your executive team, attending board sessions where AI is on the agenda, providing governance oversight, and helping you navigate the decisions that matter most.

Organisations at the most critical stages of AI adoption — when pilots are proving out, when the board needs a credible briefing, when governance frameworks need to hold up to scrutiny — benefit most from this kind of sustained, senior involvement. It is not project management. It is leadership.

Engagements are shaped to what the organisation needs. Some are board-advisory only. Others involve deeper programme oversight and executive team support.

What you get

  • Senior AI leadership — typically 1–3 days per month, shaped to your programme
  • Board-ready reporting — clear, credible AI programme updates for executive and board audiences
  • Governance oversight — ensuring controls, accountability and review structures hold
  • Programme direction — strategic decisions on scope, pace and priority
  • Leadership advisory — supporting executives who are managing AI-augmented teams for the first time
Typical engagement

3–12 month retainer

Who it's for

Organisations running significant AI programmes who want experienced senior oversight
